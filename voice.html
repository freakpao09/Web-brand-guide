Writing

Voice & Tone

SEJ has reported on search since 2003. The voice is confident, journalistic, and practical — the industry's trusted editor, not a cheerleader. We report, we explain, we prescribe.

Voice characteristics

Confident

Declarative sentences. We state what the data shows and what readers should do with it.

Practical

Every piece ships with how-tos, frameworks, or screenshots. Theory without application doesn't run.

Journalistic

Third-person reporting in headlines. Sources cited. Numbers double-checked.

Sober

No emoji, no hype, no hedging. The work is interesting on its own — we don't need to sell it.

Writing guidelines

Do

  • Use Title Case for headlines, per AP style
  • Lead with the news: what changed, who's affected, what to do
  • Use ampersands to compress headlines — it reads editorial
  • Cite numbers and sources: "68M AI crawler visits across 100 sites"
  • Address readers as "you" in marketing copy, "we" when speaking as SEJ
  • Use em-dashes and colons to stack two ideas together

Don't

  • Use emoji — ever — in headlines, CTAs, nav, or copy
  • Hedge with "might possibly maybe" — be direct or don't publish
  • Write cutesy marketing fluff ("unlock your potential")
  • Use exclamation marks outside newsletter CTAs
  • Disguise ads as editorial — always label "Advertisement"
  • Overuse jargon without a plain-language explanation

Tone by surface

Editorial reporting

Journalistic
News and analysis on algorithm shifts, platform updates, and industry changes. Third person. Sources cited.
"Google confirmed Tuesday that the March core update has fully rolled out. Publishers in the YMYL space reported the most volatility — three of the five sites we tracked lost more than 40% of organic traffic within 72 hours."

Sponsorship & media kit

Direct
Sales materials speaking to media buyers and partners. Address the reader as "you". Transparent on pricing.
"Let your brand be the first thing our readers see. Reach 1.5M monthly pageviews of manager-level marketers. Starting at $5,000/week — we take care of all the promo."

Newsletter invitation

Inviting
The SEJ Today CTA. Warm but restrained — the subscribe prompt earns the click on substance, not excitement.
"The news, guides, and frameworks 70K+ marketing pros use to stay ahead. Weekdays, 7am ET. Subscribe — it's free."

Product support

Helpful
When a reader hits a paywall, a broken link, or a confusing control. Acknowledge, orient, offer the next step.
"Looks like this link expired. Here's the full archive of Ask an SEO columns — or drop us a note and we'll send the original file."
Grammar & style

Capitalization

  • Title Case for headlines (AP style — small words lowercase)
  • Title Case for navigation and button labels
  • ALL CAPS only for category eyebrows, with wide tracking
  • Sentence case for decks, captions, and UI copy

Punctuation

  • Oxford comma, always
  • Ampersands in headlines are welcomed — editorial shorthand
  • Em-dashes and colons to stack two ideas; avoid parentheses
  • No periods in button labels or single-line UI text

Numbers

  • Spell out one through nine; numerals for 10 and up
  • Use abbreviations in marketing: 1.5M, 70K+, $25,000
  • Commas in numbers over 999 (1,240 not 1240)
  • Lead with the number when it carries the story

Formatting

  • Bold for emphasis, not italics
  • Monospace for code, URLs, and technical references
  • Paragraphs stay short — three to four sentences
  • Bylines: "By First Last" with the author's 65×65 circle
